In this first part, we will get started with some basics.

Why use text to speech?

When people ask me about the one technology people with dyslexia should use, my response is text-to-speech. But in fact, everyone should use it.

Key reasons for using text-to-speech



You can rest your eyes when you get tired. Simply close your eyes and continue reading.
Listening can increase your comprehension because you are adding a new channel for information.
If you listen while you are reading you can focus on reading for longer.
Spell checks only catch misspellings. But after a while you cannot see any of the other clumsy sentences you left in. Listening to your own text reveals many things you can fix.
If you need to read a long report or something interesting from the web but don’t have time, you can convert it into an MP3 file and listen while on the go.

Other questions and answers

Q: But isn’t reading better than listening? A: No! We already addressed this in the first Tech Thursday on audio books. You can learn as much by reading as listening?Q: But aren’t computer voices just too mechanical?A: Not anymore! Even the voices that come preinstalled on most machines are pretty good. But you can buy some really great voices. The important thing is that after a while you get used to any voices and just keep reaping benefits. Q: Is there any research that shows the benefits of text-to-speech?A: Yes, there is research that shows huge benefits from using text-to-speech for people with dyslexia and everyone else. In most situations, it is as good as a real voice.

How to start with text to speech?
Add Speak button to Office

If you have Microsoft Office 2007 and later, you can make it speak to you right now without installing anything.

Simply add the Speak button to your toolbar. It is easy but requires that you follow some instructions. See here for details.

Then just select some text and click Speak.

This works in Word, Outlook, Excel and PowerPoint.

Try some free software

If you don’t use Microsoft Office, you will need to download some software. Don’t worry, there are plenty of free options. We will have a look at some of these in more detail next time but for now, you can’t go wrong with installing the Free version of Natural Reader.

After install, do this:

1-Open Natural Reader

2-Click on the Floating bar button (bottom right corner)

3-Select any text and press the play button

Get a voice

Voices are like fonts. You can install them on your computer and then any software capable of speech can use them to speak to you.

Any computer will come with at least one voice preinstalled. On Windows 7, the default voice is Anna. It is usable but not great. On Windows 8, the default voice Hazel is excellent and can compete with many commercial voices.

But if you’d like more variety, you can try some free voices or you can purchase a commercial voice.

Natural Reader will come preinstalled with a trial of some higher quality voices. But you can also go to a company like Ivona and install a trial of one of their voices.
