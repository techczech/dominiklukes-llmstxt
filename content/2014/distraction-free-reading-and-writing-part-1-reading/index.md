Why distraction-free reading?

Everybody knows that if you add too much extra information in your field of vision, you are putting obstacles in the way of understanding what you read. This makes it more difficult to process information and much easier to get distracted. That’s why it’s important to remove as many distractions from sight when reading or writing.

This is doubly important for people with literacy difficulties who may already struggle with processing speed and also have attention issues.

What is distraction-free reading?

When you get to a web page you mostly see mostly things that have nothing to do with what the article is about! They are certainly useful ways of discovering where to go next but make focusing on reading difficult. But there is a way to remove all the clutter. Distraction free or readability plugins.

There are many free readability plugins available for most browsers. As always I recommend Chrome or Firefox over Internet Explorer. But if you cannot or do not want to install a plug in you can also use something called bookmarklet.

What does a readability plugin do?

A basic readability plugin will:

Strip away everything from a page except the main article
Leave key images in the text but remove anything else not related to the text
Choose font, size, background colour and margins (options are typically limited)
Work only on pages with articles, not a home page of a site without main text
Create a printer-friendly version of a page

Some readability plugins will also let you save a page for reading later by:

Saving it in its own web archive
Sending it to your tablet or phone (you need to install an app)
Sending to your Kindle

How do bookmarklets work

Most readability plugins also work as bookmarklets.

Bookmarklets are specially formatted links that contain code. Almost any code you can use on a website can be hidden in a bookmarklet. So a bookmarklet is a little app. Simply save a bookmarklet in your bookmarks and favourites and click on it when you want to run the app. For example, when you’re on a page you want to make readable.

Bookmarklets don’t have to be installed so you can use them in any browser that allows bookmarks or favourites.

Some readability plugins to try
Clearly

Clearly is my readability plug in of choice. It offers all the distraction-free reading features but also lets me save a page or a part of a page to Evernote. I use Evernote to keep notes about what I read, so this is the perfect combination. You can then read it on your phone or tablet, as well.

Clearly is the only readability plugin to offer text to speech. Unfortunately, this feature is only available to Evernote Premium users. The premium subscription costs £4 a month. There are other free text-to-speech options, so this feature is not probably worth it on it’s own.

Another unique feature is highlighting of parts of the text and it has the most options for customising how the page is displayed.

Clearly is available for Chrome, Firefox and Opera.

Readability

Readability is a free service created by the people who pioneered the concept of readability in 2008. Today, it offers the whole package of possible features for a readability app. It will:

Remove distractions from a webpage
Save the page for reading later in the Readability archive
Send the page to your Kindle
Display a readable version of your page on your phone or tablet

Readability is also your best bet if you cannot install a plugin because it also offers a bookmarklet.

The reason I don’t use Readability is because I find it a little slow. It is still usable but takes a few seconds to convert a page where others are almost instant.

Two best-known alternatives to the Save for later function of Readability are Pocket and Instapaper. But, with these, you have to save the page first before you can view the distraction-free version.

iReader

iReader was my favourite before I switched to Clearly. It is an old school readability plugin with no need for a third party service. It works in the browser only and does only one thing: readability. However, it is much faster than the other two.

I particularly like the toolbar that appears only when you hover at the bottom of the page.

It is the only plugin that will let you remove all images from the text.

Its main disadvantage is that it does not let you change the background colour.

You can download iReader for Chrome or iReader for Firefox.
